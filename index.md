# Privacy Policy — PrivateNav

**Last updated:** March 4, 2026

## Summary

Your data stays on your device. We don't collect, store, or share any personal information.

## What the App Does

PrivateNav helps you plan an optimal multi-stop driving route while keeping your personal data private. It uses your addresses to calculate the best driving order — but your identity never leaves your device.

## Data Handling

### Contact Names & Addresses
- Contact names and addresses you enter or import from your phone's Contacts **stay entirely on your device**.
- Names are **never sent** to any external service.
- Only **geographic coordinates** (latitude/longitude) are sent to Google Maps APIs for route calculation — not street addresses, not names, not any identifying information.

### Google Maps API
- The app uses Google Geocoding, Routes, and Elevation APIs to convert addresses to coordinates and calculate driving routes.
- These API calls send only coordinates, not personal identifiers.
- Google's privacy policy applies to their API services: [Google Privacy Policy](https://policies.google.com/privacy)

### Data Storage
- Recipient list: stored locally on your device as a JSON file.
- API key: stored in the iOS Keychain (encrypted).
- Settings: stored in local app preferences.
- **No data is stored on any server. There is no backend, no analytics, no tracking.**

### No Data Collection
- No analytics or crash reporting SDKs.
- No advertising.
- No user accounts or authentication.
- No data transmitted to the developer.

## Permissions

| Permission | Purpose | When Used |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| Contacts | Import delivery addresses from your contacts | Only when you tap "Import from Contacts" |

Contact access is optional. The app works without it.

## Third-Party Services

The only third-party service used is **Google Maps Platform** (Geocoding, Routes, Elevation APIs) for route calculation. No other third-party services, SDKs, or trackers are included.

## Children's Privacy

This app does not knowingly collect any information from children under 13.
